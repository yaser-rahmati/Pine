B. What Is a Simple Moving Average (SMA)?
	1. Simple moving averages calculate the average of a range of prices by the number of periods within that range.
	2. A simple moving average is a technical indicator that can aid in determining if an asset price will continue or if it will reverse a bull or bear trend.
	3. A simple moving average can be enhanced as an exponential moving average (EMA) that is more heavily weighted on recent price action.
	


C. Formula 
	1. SMA = (A1 + A2 + ... + An)/n
	2. n=the number of total periods
	3. An = The price of an asset at period n

D. Script

// © yaser_rahmati
//@version=4
study("YAS-SMA", overlay = true)

len = input(defval=5 , title="Length" , type=input.integer , minval=1)
src = input(defval=close , title="Source" , type=input.source)
clr = input(defval=color.red , title="Color" , type=input.color)
ofs = input(defval=0 , title="Offset" , minval=-500 , maxval=500 , tooltip="Positive: shift to Right; Negative: Shift to Left")
thik = input(defval=2 , title="Thickness" , minval=1 , maxval=4)

sum = 0.0
for i=0 to len-1
	sum := sum + src[i]
mva = sum / len

plot(mva , color=clr , offset=ofs , linewidth=thik)
